Research

I am interested in a range of applied topics related to research communication, statistics, and mathematics education. Some of my recent research projects have focused on:

  • Examining effective methods for communicating research findings to educators.
  • Evaluating the informativeness of large-scale randomized controlled trials (RCTs) in education.
  • Identifying and addressing common misconceptions in fraction and decimal arithmetic.

I also have interest meta-analytical techniques (and their limitations), and I am committed to open-sciences practices. If you share similar interests, feel free to reach out.

Teaching

I am currently responsible for teaching the following courses:

  • Statistics for Large Data (MAC171/MAP103)
  • Statistics and Data Analysis (NTA201)
  • Evidence in Education (LNC202)

I also supervise PhD students in Mathematics education, as well as graduate students who are conducting dissertations in data sciences.
